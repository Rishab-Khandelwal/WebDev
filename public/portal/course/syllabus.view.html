Northeastern University CS5610 - Web Development

Course Description

Discusses Web development for sites that are dynamic, data driven, and interactive. Focuses on the software development issues of integrating multiple languages, assorted data technologies, and Web interaction. Considers ASP.NET, C#, HTTP, HTML, CSS, XML, XSLT, JavaScript, AJAX, RSS/Atom, SQL, and Web services. Each student must deploy individually designed Web experiments that illustrate the Web technologies and at least one major integrative Web site project. Students may work in teams with the permission of the instructor. Each student or team must also create extensive documentation of their goals, plans, design decisions, accomplishments, and user guidelines. All source files must be open and be automatically served by a sources server.

Course Expectations

Assignments

There will be six (6) assignments that will help you reinforce the topics discussed in class. Assignments will be submitted through a public code repository available to the instructor and TAs. Assignments must also be hosted online on a public server where they can fully validated. The hosted version must match the lastest version in the source control.

Project

For this course you are expected to create a fully functional Web application on a topic of your choice. There will be six (6) project related assignments that will guide you through the various steps of working on your project. The first assignment will be to discuss and decide the topic and scope of your assignment with your instructor. The project will be hosted on a public server of your choice and fully working by the end of the semester. All the source code for your project must be available to the instructor and TAs through an online repository.

Exams

There will not be any quizes or exams

Category Percent Description
Home Page 5% Your home page should have a picture of yourself, link to your assignments, link to your final project implementation on OpenShift, link to your GitHub, link to the documentation of your project, link to a 5 minute video demoing your project. Participation 5% Ways to participate in class are ask questions in class, ask/answer question on Piazza. I will use Doodle.com to schedule appointments for office hours. Make sure to come if you do schedule an appointment. Go to the TA office hours.
Sustained Effort 5% Invite TAs and instructors to your Git repositories. They will keep track of your progress throughout the semester. Your commit and push history should illustrate a steady, sustained effort through a consistent stream of commits. We don't want to see sparse, intense activity with long periods of inactivity. Your commits should be small and frequent, with meaningful comments about your progress.
Assignments 40% Make sure you fulfill all requirements listed in the assignment document, general course requirements in the course videos, and lectures. Submit assignments by the due date tagging them appropriately. Submission date will be based on the date the tag was applied to the commit. Late submissions will lose 1% per hour it is late, e.g., an assignment 15 hours late will receive a 15% penalty. If you need to resubmit the assignment, simply use the same tag in a later commit you wish graded.
Project 45% Make sure you fulfill all requirements listed in the project requirements document, general course requirements in the course videos, and lectures. Submit project by the due date tagging it appropriately. If you need to resubmit the project, simply use the same tag in a later commit you wish graded. You can keep working after the due date. You may be graded at any time after the due date, but you must stop IF asked by your TA or instructor. You might not get a message to stop.
